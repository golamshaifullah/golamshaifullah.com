The MeerKAT telescope as a pulsar facility: System verification and early science results from MeerTime

July, 2020
PDF Cite DOI

Abstract

We describe system verification tests and early science results from the pulsar processor &lpar;PTUSE&rpar; developed for the newly commissioned 64-dish SARAO MeerKAT radio telescope in South Africa. MeerKAT is a high-gain &lpar; &amp;dollar;&lcub;&amp;sim;&rcub;2.8 &bsol;mbox&lcub;K Jy&rcub;&Hat;&lcub;-1&rcub;&amp;dollar; &rpar; low-system temperature &lpar; &amp;dollar;&lcub;&amp;sim;&rcub;18 &bsol;mbox&lcub;K at &rcub;20 &bsol;mbox&lcub;cm&rcub;&amp;dollar; &rpar; radio array that currently operates at 580-1 670 MHz and can produce tied-array beams suitable for pulsar observations. This paper presents results from the MeerTime Large Survey Project and commissioning tests with PTUSE. Highlights include observations of the double pulsar &amp;dollar;&bsol;mbox&lcub;J&rcub;0737&lcub;-&rcub;3039&bsol;mbox&lcub;A&rcub;&amp;dollar; , pulse profiles from 34 millisecond pulsars &lpar;MSPs&rpar; from a single 2.5-h observation of the Globular cluster Terzan 5, the rotation measure of Ter5O, a 420-sigma giant pulse from the Large Magellanic Cloud pulsar PSR &amp;dollar;&bsol;mbox&lcub;J&rcub;0540&lcub;-&rcub;6919&amp;dollar; , and nulling identified in the slow pulsar PSR J0633-2015. One of the key design specifications for MeerKAT was absolute timing errors of less than 5 ns using their novel precise time system. Our timing of two bright MSPs confirm that MeerKAT delivers exceptional timing. PSR &amp;dollar;&bsol;mbox&lcub;J&rcub;2241&lcub;-&rcub;5236&amp;dollar; exhibits a jitter limit of &amp;dollar;&amp;lt;4 &bsol;mbox&lcub;ns h&rcub;&Hat;&lcub;-1&rcub;&amp;dollar; whilst timing of PSR &amp;dollar;&bsol;mbox&lcub;J&rcub;1909&lcub;-&rcub;3744&amp;dollar; over almost 11 months yields an rms residual of 66 ns with only 4 min integrations. Our results confirm that the MeerKAT is an exceptional pulsar telescope. The array can be split into four separate sub-arrays to time over 1 000 pulsars per day and the future deployment of S-band &lpar;1 750-3 500 MHz&rpar; receivers will further enhance its capabilities.
